## üéØ Deal of the Week
### **Stonepeak acquires 65% stake in BP's Castrol ‚Äì $10.1bn**

**Transaction Overview**
- **Deal Value:** $10.1bn (Enterprise Value)
- **Structure:** 65% majority controlling interest; all-cash
- **Multiple:** ~8.6x EV/LTM EBITDA
- **Sector:** Energy / Lubricants
- **Geography:** London, UK / New York, US

**Strategic Rationale**
- **Portfolio High-Grading:** This divestment is a core pillar of BP‚Äôs $20 billion program to simplify its portfolio and reduce net debt toward a $14-18 billion target by 2027.
- **Infrastructure Arbitrage:** Stonepeak is capitalizing on the transition of "essential service" assets into private infrastructure funds, leveraging its massive AUM to drive operational efficiencies in a business with nine consecutive quarters of growth.
- **Retention of Upside:** By retaining a 35% minority interest, BP maintains exposure to Castrol's growth while Stonepeak assumes the heavy capital requirements for global expansion.

**Analyst Take**
> This deal highlights the "Deleveraging Cycle" defining late 2025. Strategic acquirers are no longer buying for pure scale; they are buying for balance sheet hygiene. At an 8.6x multiple, Stonepeak has secured a resilient, cash-generative machine at a significant discount to broader specialty chemical valuations. Expect 2026 to be defined by similar "Big Oil" carve-outs as majors race to fund energy transition projects with legacy assets.

## üîç Deep Dive Insight
### **The $2.1 Trillion Great Credit Migration**
**What‚Äôs Happening** :
A structural power shift has culminated in 2025: over $2.1 trillion has migrated from traditional bank vaults into private credit and infrastructure funds. As traditional banks play defense due to Basel IV constraints, private capital has moved to the "offensive," displacing banks in even the largest acquisition financings.

**Why It Matters**
- **Certainty Over Speed:** In the current rate environment, private credit offers customization and execution certainty that volatile bond markets cannot match.
- **The "Physical AI" Play:** This capital migration is fueling the "Infrastructure Arbitrage" seen in data center deals. Acquirers are no longer buying software "hype"; they are buying the energy-heavy hard assets (GW capacity) that make the AI economy possible.
